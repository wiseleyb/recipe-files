<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Green_Curry-Mango_Sauce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Green Curry-Mango Sauce">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> 2 tablespoons plus 1/2 cup canola oil 
</li><li> 1 red onion, coarsely chopped 
</li><li> 3 cloves garlic, chopped 
</li><li> 1 tablespoon chopped fresh ginger 
</li><li> 1 serrano chile, coarsely chopped 
</li><li> 2 tablespoons green curry paste 
</li><li> 3 ripe mangoes, peeled, pitted, and coarsely chopped 
</li><li> 2 cups water 
</li><li> 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice 
</li><li> 3 tablespoons chopped cilantro leaves 
</li><li> 1 tablespoon honey 
</li><li> Salt and freshly ground pepper
</li></ul>
<p><br />
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Green_Curry-Mango_Sauce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Green Curry-Mango Sauce">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add onion and cook until soft, about 3 minutes. Add garlic, ginger, and serrano chile and cook for 1 minute. Add the curry paste and cook for 2 minutes. Add mangoes and water, stir well, and cook until the mango is soft, about 15 to 20 minutes. Transfer the mixture to a blender (see *Cook's Note below). Add the lime juice, cilantro, and honey and blend until smooth. With the motor running, slowly add the remaining 1/2 cup oil and blend until emulsified. Season with salt and pepper, to taste. <br />
Cook's Note: When blending hot liquids, remove liquid from the heat and allow to cool for at least 5 minutes. Transfer liquid to a blender or food processor and fill it no more than halfway. If using a blender, release 1 corner of the lid. This prevents the vacuum effect that creates heat explosions. Place a towel over the top of the machine, pulse a few times, and then process on high speed until smooth.
