<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Pasta_with_Simple_White_sauce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Pasta with Simple White sauce">edit</a>]</div><a name="Credits"></a><h2>Credits</h2>
<p>by Ben
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Pasta_with_Simple_White_sauce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Pasta with Simple White sauce">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> Spaghetti (two servings - about half a box)
</li><li> 1 egg 
</li><li> kosher salt
</li><li> 1/2 (or more) cup parmesan
</li><li> 1/2 stick butter
</li><li> ground pepper
</li><li> oven proof mixing bowl (metal, ceramic)
</li></ul>
<p><br />
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Pasta_with_Simple_White_sauce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Pasta with Simple White sauce">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>Preheat oven to 250<br />
Place bowl in oven with butter (so it melts)<br />
Preheat pasta bowls as well<br />
<br />
Boil pasta in heavily salted water (salting water very important - as the pasta flavor is a major component)<br />
<br />
Beat the egg in a bowl while pasta is boiling<br />
<br />
Right before pasta is done cooking reserve 1 cup of it's boiling water (pasta water will have taste and the starch in it is used a thickening agent)<br />
<br />
Drain pasta and immediately move it to the bowl that's been in the oven with th butter. <br />
<br />
Quickly toss and slowly add parmesan cheese and beaten egg.<br />
<br />
If it's too dry add a bit of the pasta water.<br />
<br />
Season with more parm and black pepper.  Serve in preheated bowls.<br />
<br />
If you're really lazy about dishes you could use the pot that you boiled the pasta in for mixing and melt the butter in the microwave.
