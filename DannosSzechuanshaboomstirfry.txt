        Danno's Szechuan shaboom stir-fry      </h1>

        <p><br />
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Danno%27s_Szechuan_shaboom_stir-fry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Danno's Szechuan shaboom stir-fry">edit</a>]</div><a name="Credits"></a><h2>Credits</h2>
<p>by Centre Street Cafe - JP, Boston
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Danno%27s_Szechuan_shaboom_stir-fry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Danno's Szechuan shaboom stir-fry">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> 1  tablespoon cornstarch
</li><li> 3/4 cup water
</li><li> 1/2 tablespoon soy sauce
</li><li> 1/2 tablespoon dark sesame oil (from an Asian market)
</li><li> 1/2 tablespoon chopped fresh ginger root
</li><li> 1 clove garlic, chopped
</li><li> 1 tablespoon chili garlic sauce (such as Tuong Ot Toi Vietnam brand)
</li><li> 1 to 2 tablespoons peanut oil
</li><li> 5 cups assorted chopped or julienned vegetables (broccoli florets, onions, zucchini, snow peas, carrots, red and green bell peppers, Napa cabbage, bean sprouts)
</li><li> 1/4 pound fresh lo mein or soba noodles, cooked according to package directions and drained
</li><li> 1/2 tablespoon sesame seeds, toasted
</li><li> 3 scallions, finely chopped
</li></ul>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Danno%27s_Szechuan_shaboom_stir-fry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Danno's Szechuan shaboom stir-fry">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>1. In a bowl stir together the cornstarch and 1/4 cup water.
</p><p>2. In another bowl, combine the remaining 1/2 cup water, soy sauce, sesame oil, ginger, garlic, and chili sauce.
</p><p>3. In a wok or large skillet, add enough peanut oil to the pan to barely coat the bottom.
</p><p>4. Add the vegetables. Cook, stirring, until broccoli turns emerald. Remove from the pan; keep warm.
</p><p>5. Pour the soy sauce mixture into the skillet and bring to a boil. Mix the cornstarch and water again. Stir half of it into the pan. If you want a thicker sauce, add the remaining cornstarch.
</p><p>6. Return the vegetables to the pan. Toss over high heat. Add the noodles and toss again.
</p><p>7. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and scallions.
</p><p>Adapted from Centre Street Cafe
</p>
