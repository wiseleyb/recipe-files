<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Crab_Cakes_with_Thai_Vinaigrette&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Crab Cakes with Thai Vinaigrette">edit</a>]</div><a name="Credits"></a><h2>Credits</h2>
<p><a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/food/recipes/recipe/0,,FOOD_9936_2727,00.html" title="http://www.foodnetwork.com/food/recipes/recipe/0,,FOOD 9936 2727,00.html" class="external text" rel="nofollow">Original Recipe</a>  by Fredric A. Byarm
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Crab_Cakes_with_Thai_Vinaigrette&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Crab Cakes with Thai Vinaigrette">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> 1/4 head radicchio 
</li><li> 1/2 carrot 
</li><li> 1/2 red pepper 
</li><li> 1/2 yellow pepper 
</li><li> 1/2 green pepper 
</li><li> 1 head endive 
</li><li> 1 tablespoon minced cilantro 
</li><li> 1 tablespoon minced parsley 
</li><li> 2 cups Panko bread crumbs 
</li><li> 1 egg yolk 
</li><li> 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard 
</li><li> 3 limes 
</li><li> 2 ounces of fresh ginger 
</li><li> 1 cup of safflower oil or vegetable oil 
</li><li> 1 pound jumbo lump crab meat 
</li><li> 2 leafy stems on celery for garnish
</li><li> Thai Vinaigrette: 
</li><li> 1 tablespoons of red curry paste 
</li><li> Juice of 1/2 lemon 
</li><li> 1 shallot 
</li><li> 1/2 cup of safflower oil or vegetable oil plus 1 tablespoon
</li></ul>
<p><br />
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Crab_Cakes_with_Thai_Vinaigrette&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Crab Cakes with Thai Vinaigrette">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>Dice radicchio, carrot, red pepper, yellow pepper, green pepper, endive, cilantro and parsley, and place in a large mixing bowl. In a food processor, pulse one cup of Panko bread crumbs until very fine. Set aside. Add the other cup of Panko to the vegetable mixture. <br />
In a mixer, add egg yolk, Dijon mustard, turn mixer on medium, incorporate ingredients then add juice of 3 limes and ginger. Turn mixer on high and incorporate oil slowly. Emulsify until of mayonnaise consistency. Add this to vegetable mixture. <br />
<br />
Pick through jumbo lump crab meat checking for shells, add crab meat to vegetable mixture. Mix all ingredients well. Try to preserve the shape of crab meat. <br />
<br />
Form 3 ounce crab cakes from mixture. Then coat each crab cake in the fine bread crumbs. Set aside until ready to saute.<br />
<br />
Thai Vinaigrette: <br />
<br />
In a blender add red curry paste, lemon juice and shallot. Turn blender on high slowly adding oil until emulsified then place in a squirt bottle for plating. In saute pan saute crabcakes, two crabcakes per person brown on one side. Flip over and place saute pan in a 375 degree oven for five minutes. <br />
On plate make a design with vinaigrette, place cooked crab cakes on top and serve. Garnish with celery stems.
