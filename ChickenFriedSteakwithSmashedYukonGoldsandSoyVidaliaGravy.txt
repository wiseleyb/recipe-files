</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Chicken_Fried_Steak_with_Smashed_Yukon_Golds_and_Soy-Vidalia_Gravy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Chicken Fried Steak with Smashed Yukon Golds and Soy-Vidalia Gravy">edit</a>]</div><a name="Credits"></a><h2>Credits</h2>
<p><a href="http://www.foodtv.com/foodtv/recipe/0,6255,13833,00.html" title="http://www.foodtv.com/foodtv/recipe/0,6255,13833,00.html" class="external text" rel="nofollow">Original Recipe</a>  by MingTsai
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Chicken_Fried_Steak_with_Smashed_Yukon_Golds_and_Soy-Vidalia_Gravy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Chicken Fried Steak with Smashed Yukon Golds and Soy-Vidalia Gravy">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> 1 cup canola oil 
</li><li> 4 (8-ounce) cubed steaks (top or bottom round ends)
</li><li> Salt and black pepper, to taste
</li><li> 1 cup all-purpose flour
</li><li> 1 tablespoon dried thyme
</li><li> 1 tablespoon dried basil 
</li><li> Chicken fat or vegetable oil for frying
</li><li> 3 Vidalia onions, sliced
</li><li> 1/2 pound paris mushrooms, prepped and sliced
</li><li> 1 cup dry red wine
</li><li> 2 tablespoon naturally brewed soy sauce
</li><li> 2 cups chicken stock
</li><li> 1/4 cup chives, chopped for garnish
</li><li> 8 large Yukon potatoes, washed, baked in the oven until a fork comes cleanly off, 350 degrees, about 45 minutes
</li></ul>
<p><br />
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Chicken_Fried_Steak_with_Smashed_Yukon_Golds_and_Soy-Vidalia_Gravy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Chicken Fried Steak with Smashed Yukon Golds and Soy-Vidalia Gravy">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>In a large heavy skillet, heat the canola oil on high heat. Season the steaks with salt and pepper. On a plate, mix the flour, thyme, and basil. Dredge the steaks in the flour and fry in canola oil, about 4 to 5 minutes a side, until brown. Set steaks aside to rest and remove all but 3 tablespoons canola oil. Add enough seasoned flour until it becomes thick, like soft clay (roux), and stir with a whisk for 3 to 4 minutes until smooth and cooked through. Remove roux. In the same skillet, add the onions and mushrooms, season and brown well, 8 to 10 minutes. Deglaze with red wine and reduce completely. Add the soy and chicken stock and bring to a boil. Whisk in 1 tablespoon of roux until thick. If not thickened enough, whisk in more roux. Check for flavor. <br />
<br />
PLATING On a dinner plate, place 2 potatoes down, split open and smother with gravy. Lean 1 steak next to the potatoes and garnish with chives.
