<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Key_Lime_Pie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Key Lime Pie">edit</a>]</div><a name="Credits"></a><h2>Credits</h2>
<p>by Gourmet May 2003
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Key_Lime_Pie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Key Lime Pie">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> Crust:
</li><li> 1.25 cups graham crakcker rumbs from 9 crackers
</li><li> 2 tbs sugar
</li><li> 5 tbs unsalted butter, melted
</li><li> Filling:
</li><li> 1 (14oz) can sweetened condensed milk
</li><li> 4 egg yolks
</li><li> 1/2 cup plus 2 tbs fresh or bottled Key lime juice (Manhattan brand)
</li><li> Topping:
</li><li> 3/4 cup heavy cream
</li></ul>
<p><br />
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Key_Lime_Pie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Key Lime Pie">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>Make Crust:<br />
Preheat oven to 350<br />
<br />
Stir together graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and butter in a bowl with a fork until combined well, then press mixture evenly onto bottom and up side of a 9-inch (4 cup) glass pit plate.<br />
<br />
Bake crust in middle of oven 10 minutes and cool in pie plate on a rack.  Leave oven on.<br />
<br />
Make filling and bake pie:<br />
Whisk together condensed milk and yolks in a bowl until combined well.  Add juice and whisk until combined (mixture will thicken slightly).<br />
<br />
Pour filling into crustt and bake in middle of oven 15 minutes.  Cool pie completely on rack (filling will set as it cools), then chill, covered, at least 8 hours.<br />
<br />
Make topping:<br />
Just before serving, beat cream in a bowl with an electric mixer until it just holds stiff peaks.  Serve pie topped with cream.<br />
<br />
Pie, without topping, can be chilled up to 1 day.
</p><p>Notes:  Key limes are also known as Mexican or West Indian limes.  If you can't find them in your area substitue bottled Key lime juice.  Gourmet suggests Manhattan brand.
