<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Beef_Stew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Beef Stew">edit</a>]</div><a name="Credits"></a><h2>Credits</h2>
<p>by Greg Atkinson
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Beef_Stew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Beef Stew">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> 1/2 cup olive oil
</li><li> 1/2 cup flour
</li><li> 2 lbs beef stew meat, cut into 1-inch cubes
</li><li> 1 small onion, peeled and finely chopped
</li><li> 4 cloves garlic, peeled
</li><li> pinch of dried thyme
</li><li> black pepper
</li><li> 1 can (22 ounces) tomatoes
</li><li> 1 can (17 ounces) beef broth
</li><li> 1 cup red wine or coffee
</li><li> 3 medium carrots, peeled and sliced 1/8 inch thick
</li><li> 4 large thin-skinned potatoes, such as White Rose or Yukon Gold, cut into 1/2 inch cubes
</li><li> 1 package (10 ounces) frozen peas
</li><li> salt to taste
</li></ul>
<p><br />
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Beef_Stew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Beef Stew">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>In a large stew pot with a heavy base, cook oil and flour over medium-high heat, stirring constantly, for 6 to 8 minutes, or until flour is nicely browned.  (If left unstirred for even a moment, the flour may burn; if it does, discard the roux and begin again.)  When you have a smooth, evenly browned base with no black specks, stir in beef.  Add onion, garlic, thyme, pepper, tomatoes, beef broth, and wine or coffee.  Brin the liquid to a boil, reduce heat to medium low, and cook 1 hour, stirring from time to time to prevent sticking.<br />
<br />
Add carrots, potatoes, and peas and cook 30 minutes longer, or until potatoes are tender.  Taste and add salt as desired.
</p><p>Notes:<br />
I often just use beef and 1 potatoe and leave out the carrots and peas.
