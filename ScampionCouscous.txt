<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Scampi_on_Couscous&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Scampi on Couscous">edit</a>]</div><a name="Credits"></a><h2>Credits</h2>
<p><a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/food/recipes/recipe/0,,FOOD_9936_23248,00.html" title="http://www.foodnetwork.com/food/recipes/recipe/0,,FOOD 9936 23248,00.html" class="external text" rel="nofollow">Original Recipe</a>  by Giada De Laurentiis
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Scampi_on_Couscous&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Scampi on Couscous">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> 1/2 cup plus 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil 
</li><li> 1 small onion, chopped 
</li><li> 1 carrot, chopped 
</li><li> 3 garlic cloves, peeled and minced 
</li><li> 2 (8 ounce) cans chopped tomatoes in their juice 
</li><li> 1 (8ounce) bottle clam juice 
</li><li> 1/4 cup dry white wine 
</li><li> 1 cup water 
</li><li> 2 cups plain couscous 
</li><li> 2 pounds large shrimp, peeled and deveined 
</li><li> 1 lemon, juiced 
</li><li> 1 tablespoon red pepper flakes 
</li><li> Chopped parsley leaves, for garnish
</li></ul>
<p><br />
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Scampi_on_Couscous&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Scampi on Couscous">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>In a large pot, heat 1/4 cup olive oil. When almost smoking, add onion, carrot and garlic and saute until vegetables are soft, about 5 minutes. Add the canned tomatoes and their juice, clam juice and white wine. Bring to a boil and simmer on medium heat for 10 minutes, uncovered. Remove from heat and allow to cool slightly. Carefully pour tomato mixture in the bowl of a food processor and puree. Add a couple of tablespoons of water if needed - you want to end up with a broth. Check for seasoning. <br />
Return broth to the pot. Add 1 cup of water and 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and add 2 cups couscous. Cover pot and remove from heat. Let rest for 10 minutes, allowing the couscous to absorb all the liquid. Fluff with a fork and season with salt and pepper. <br />
<br />
In a large skillet, heat the remaining 1/4 cup oil. Add shrimp and stirring occasionally, cook the shrimp until they start to turn pink, about 5 minutes. Be careful not to overcook the shrimp or they will become tough. Remove from heat and add the lemon juice, red pepper and chopped parsley. Check for seasoning. <br />
<br />
To serve, mound the couscous in the center of a platter and top with the shrimp.
