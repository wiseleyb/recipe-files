<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Eight-Alarm_Chili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Eight-Alarm Chili">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> 2 oz dried ancho chiles stemmed and seeded
</li><li> 6 large garlic cloves, 3 of them finely chopped
</li><li> 1 tbs salt
</li><li> 1.5 ground cumin
</li><li> 1.5 tbs chili powder
</li><li> 4 lb well-marbled beef brisket or boneless chuck, trimmed, cut into 1-2 inch pieces
</li><li> 3-4 tbs vegetable oil
</li><li> 1 (28-32 oz) can whole tomatoes in juice
</li><li> 1/4 cup canned chipolte chiles in adobo
</li><li> 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
</li><li> 1.5 lbs white onions, chopped (4 cups)
</li><li> 1 tbs dried oregano
</li><li> 1-4 fresh serrano or other small green chiles, finely chpped including seeds (include seeds for 1 for mild - 4 for insane)
</li><li> 12oz beer (not dark)
</li><li> 2 cups water
</li><li> 2.5 cups cooked pinto beans (optional, rinsed if canned)
</li><li> Accompaniments
</li><li> cubed avocado; chopped white onion; shredded cheddar; chopped cilantro; sour cream
</li></ul>
<p><br />
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Eight-Alarm_Chili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Eight-Alarm Chili">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>Soak ancho chiles in hot water to cover until softened, about 30 minutes.  Drain well.<br />
<br />
While chiles soak, mine 1 whole garlic clove and mash to a paste with 1/2 tbs salt, 1/2 tbs cumin, and 1/2 tbs chili powder.  Pat beef dry and toss with spice mixture in a large bowl until covered.<br />
<br />
Heat 2 tbs oil in a wide 6-7 qt heavy pot over moderately high heat until hot but not smoking, then brown beef in 3 or 4 batches, without crowding, turning occasionally, about 5 minutes per batch (lower heat if spices begin to burn).  Transfer beef as brownded to another bowl (Do not clean pot).<br />
<br />
Puree anchos in a blender along with tomatoes (uincluding juice), chipoltes in adobo, cilantro, remaining 2 whole garlic cloves, and remaing 1/2 tsp salt until smooth.<br />
<br />
Add enough oil to fat in pot to total 3 tbs, then cook onions and chopped garlic over moderate heat, stirring and scraping up brown bits from beef, until softened, 8-10 minutes.  Add oregano, remaining tbs cumin, and remaining tbs chili powder and cook, stirring, 2 minutes.  Add chile puree and 1 chopped serrano and simmer for 5 minutes.  Stir in beer, water, and beef along with any juices accumulated in bowl and gently simmer, partially covered, stirring occasionally and checking often to make sure chili is not scorching, 2 hours.<br />
<br />
Taste sauce, then add more serrano if desired and contiue to simmer, partially ocvered, until beef is very tender and sauce is slighly thickened, 1 to 2 hours more.<br />
<br />
Coarsely shred meat (still in pot) with 2 forks and cool chili completely, uncovered, then chill, covered, 1 to 2 days to allow flavors to develop. <br />
<br />
Reheat for 30 minutes before serving.  If using beans add them 5 minutes before serving.
