<p>by Su-Mei Yu
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Crispy_Garlic_%28Ga-Tiem_Chew%29&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Crispy Garlic (Ga-Tiem Chew)">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> 30 garlic cloves, peeled
</li><li> 2 cups vegetable oil
</li><li> Sea salt
</li></ul>
<p><br />
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Crispy_Garlic_%28Ga-Tiem_Chew%29&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Crispy Garlic (Ga-Tiem Chew)">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>As with <a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Crispy_Shallots_%28Hom_Chew%29" title="Crispy Shallots (Hom Chew)">Crispy Shallots (Hom Chew)</a>, slow-cooking and patience are the key to golden crispy garlic.  Thai often deep-fry the papery skins together with the garlic cloves.<br />
<br />
Fir a food processor with the steel blade, add the garlic cloves, and pulse 20 to 30 times, or until the garlic is coarsely chopped. <br />
<br />
Fry the garlic, using the same technique as for making <a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Crispy_Shallots_%28Hom_Chew%29" title="Crispy Shallots (Hom Chew)">Crispy Shallots (Hom Chew)</a>.  Be sure to stir constantly, and let the garlic brown slowly until golden, 10 to 15 minutes.  Be patient; this takes longer than you think it should.  Drain as for crispy shallots, cool, and store the garlic in a jar with a tight-fitting lid at room temperature for a month.
</p>
