<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Kartoffelspitz_und_Spaetzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Kartoffelspitz und Spaetzle">edit</a>]</div><a name="Credits"></a><h2>Credits</h2>
<p>by Rainer/Heidi Suletzki
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Kartoffelspitz_und_Spaetzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Kartoffelspitz und Spaetzle">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> 2 beef leg cross sections with marrow bone-in (beef shank?)
</li><li> 2 beef Knorr -   and 1 vegetable Knorr boullion cubes
</li><li> 3 to four medium size carrots
</li><li> 1 bunch parsely
</li><li> 1/3 seleriac ( also called selery root )
</li><li> 1 package of spaetzle type noodles
</li><li> 2 lbs of potatoes ( preferably potatoes which do not fall apart when cooked )
</li><li> Salt and pepper to tast.
</li></ul>
<p><br />
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Kartoffelspitz_und_Spaetzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Kartoffelspitz und Spaetzle">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>Wash meet and clean it from resedue of the meat <br />
slicer( in other words scrape <br />
it but leave the little bit of fat attached )<br />
Place in pot and cover with water ( water to be <br />
approx. 2 inches above meat <br />
in pot )<br />
Add boullion cubes and washed parsely.<br />
Skin carrots and add them to pot contents.<br />
Peel seleriac, cut in chunks and add to pot.<br />
Bring to boil and reduce heat to simmer, - skim <br />
water surface to remove <br />
impurities.<br />
Simmer until meat is fork-tender ( approx 1.5 <br />
hours )<br />
<br />
After one hour, prepare spaetle<br />
Peel potatoes and cut in small pieces, - boil <br />
them in salted water<br />
<br />
Remove meat from broth and keep warm<br />
Sift broth through a colander after removing the <br />
seleriac and carrots, both <br />
of which can be kept warm together with the meat.<br />
<br />
Serve in separate bowls: Broth, Spaetzle, <br />
potatoes and meat.<br />
<br />
Here in Orono we help ourselves to a larger piece <br />
of meat, cut it in smaller <br />
pieces, do the same with the vegetables, add <br />
broth, potatoes and Spaetzle in <br />
the amounts we like.<br />
<br />
The only variation I can think of would be to cut <br />
the meat in small chunks <br />
and add them to the broth before you serve the <br />
dinner.<br />
<br />
PS: Heide sometimes adds a very small amount of <br />
cream to her broth, once all <br />
other ingredients have been combined in the deep <br />
dish at the table
</p><div class="printfooter">
