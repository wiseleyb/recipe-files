<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Jack_Daniels_Glaze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Jack Daniels Glaze">edit</a>]</div><a name="Credits"></a><h2>Credits</h2>
<p><a href="http://www.recipesource.com/side-dishes/sauces/10/rec1042.html" title="http://www.recipesource.com/side-dishes/sauces/10/rec1042.html" class="external text" rel="nofollow">Original Recipe</a>
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Jack_Daniels_Glaze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Jack Daniels Glaze">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> 1 head garlic
</li><li> 1 tablespoon olive oil
</li><li> 2/3 cup water
</li><li> 1 cup pineapple juice
</li><li> 1/4 cup Kikkoman teriyaki sauce
</li><li> 1 tablespoon soy sauce
</li><li> 1 1/3 cups dark brown sugar
</li><li> 3 tablespoons lemon juice
</li><li> 3 tablespoons minced white onion
</li><li> 1 tablespoon Jack Daniels Whiskey
</li><li> 1 tablespoon crushed pineapple
</li><li> 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
</li></ul>
<p><br />
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Jack_Daniels_Glaze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Jack Daniels Glaze">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>325�  Baking Pan<br />
1. Cut about 1/2-inch off of top of garlic. Cut the roots so that the garlic<br />
will sit flat. Remove the papery skin from the garlic, but leave enough so<br />
that the cloves stay together. Put garlic into a small casserole dish or<br />
baking pan, drizzle olive oil over it, and cover with a lid or foil. Bake in<br />
a preheated 325� oven for 1 hour. Remove garlic and let it cool until you<br />
can handle it.<br />
</p>
<pre><br />
</pre>
<p>2. Combine water, pineapple juice, teriyaki sauce, soy sauce, and brown<br />
sugar in a medium saucepan over medium/high heat. Stir occasionally until<br />
mixture boils then reduce heat until mixture is just simmering.<br />
</p>
<pre><br />
</pre>
<p>3. Add remaining ingredients to pan and stir. Squeeze the sides of the head<br />
of garlic until the pasty roasted garlic is squeezed out. Measure 2<br />
teaspoons into the saucepan and whisk to combine.<br />
</p>
<pre><br />
</pre>
<p>4. Let mixture simmer for 35-45 minutes or until sauce has reduced by about<br />
1/2 and is thick and syrupy. Make sure it doesn't boil over. <br />
</p>
<pre><br />
</pre>
<p>Makes 1 cup of glaze.
