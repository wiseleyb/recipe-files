<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Hot_and_Sour_Soup_Simple&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Hot and Sour Soup Simple">edit</a>]</div><a name="Credits"></a><h2>Credits</h2>
<p>by <a href="http://www.americastestkitchen.com/recipe.asp?recipeids=3222&amp;iSeason=7" title="http://www.americastestkitchen.com/recipe.asp?recipeids=3222&amp;iSeason=7" class="external text" rel="nofollow">America's Test Kitchen</a>
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Hot_and_Sour_Soup_Simple&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Hot and Sour Soup Simple">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> 7 ounces extra-firm tofu , drained
</li><li> 4 tablespoons soy sauce 
</li><li> 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil 
</li><li> 3 tablespoons cornstarch , plus an additional 1 1/2 teaspoons
</li><li> 1 boneless, center-cut, pork loin chop (1/2 inch thick, about 6 ounces), trimmed of fat and cut into 1 inch by 1/8-inch matchsticks
</li><li> 3 tablespoons cold water , plus 1 additional teaspoon
</li><li> 1 large egg 
</li><li> 6 cups low-sodium chicken broth 
</li><li> 1 cup bamboo shoots (from one 5-ounce can), sliced lengthwise into 1/8-inch-thick strips
</li><li> 4 ounces fresh shiitake mushrooms , stems removed, caps sliced 1/4 inch thick (about 1 cup)
</li><li> 5 tablespoons black Chinese vinegar or 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar plus 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar (see note above)
</li><li> 2 teaspoons chili oil (see note above)
</li><li> 1 teaspoon ground white pepper 
</li><li> 3 medium scallions , sliced thin
</li></ul>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Hot_and_Sour_Soup_Simple&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Hot and Sour Soup Simple">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>1. Place tofu in pie plate and set heavy plate on top. Weight with 2 heavy cans; let stand at least 15 minutes (tofu should release about 1/2 cup liquid). Whisk 1 tablespoon soy sauce, sesame oil, and 1 teaspoon cornstarch in medium bowl; toss pork with marinade and set aside for at least 10 minutes (but no more than 30 minutes).
</p><p>2. Combine 3 tablespoons cornstarch with 3 tablespoons water in small bowl and mix thoroughly; set aside, leaving spoon in bowl. Mix remaining 1/2 teaspoon cornstarch with remaining 1 teaspoon water in small bowl; add egg and beat with fork until combined. Set aside.
</p><p>3. Bring broth to boil in large saucepan set over medium-high heat. Reduce heat to medium-low; add bamboo shoots and mushrooms and simmer until mushrooms are just tender, about 5 minutes. While broth simmers, dice tofu into 1/2-inch cubes. Add tofu and pork, including marinade, to soup, stirring to separate any pieces of pork that stick together. Continue to simmer until pork is no longer pink, about 2 minutes.
</p><p>4. Stir cornstarch mixture to recombine. Add to soup and increase heat to medium-high; cook, stirring occasionally, until soup thickens and turns translucent, about 1 minute. Stir in vinegar, chili oil, pepper, and remaining 3 tablespoons soy sauce; turn off heat.
</p><p>5. <a href="http://www.americastestkitchen.com/recipe.asp?recipeids=3222&amp;iSeason=7#1" title="http://www.americastestkitchen.com/recipe.asp?recipeids=3222&amp;iSeason=7#1" class="external text" rel="nofollow">How To</a>. Without stirring soup, use soupspoon to slowly drizzle very thin streams of egg mixture into pot in circular motion. Let soup sit 1 minute, then return saucepan to medium-high heat. Bring soup to gentle boil, then immediately remove from heat. Gently stir soup once to evenly distribute egg; ladle into bowls and top with scallions.
</p>
