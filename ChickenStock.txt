<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Chicken_Stock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Chicken Stock">edit</a>]</div><a name="Ingredients"></a><h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul><li> 2.75kg/6lb chicken bones, wings and carcasses
</li><li> 900g/2lb white of leek
</li><li> 2 onions
</li><li> 4 stalks celery
</li><li> 175g/6oz button mushrooms
</li><li> 2 cloves garlic
</li><li> 2 sprigs parsley
</li><li> 2 sprigs fresh thyme
</li><li> 1 bay leaf
</li><li> 1tsp white peppercorns
</li></ul>
<p><br />
</p>
<div class="editsection" style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">[<a href="/recipe/index.php?title=Chicken_Stock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Chicken Stock">edit</a>]</div><a name="Directions"></a><h2>Directions</h2>
<p>Chicken stock when made should be crystal clear, jellied and a delicate pale colour. It will keep for up to 4 days in the refrigerator or try freezing it in small quantities then take out only the amount you need for a particular recipe. <br />
<br />
Finely chop the vegetables and wash them well. Remove any excess fat from the bones and rinse them well in a few changes of cold water. Place the bones in a saucepan, cover with cold water and bring to the boil. Tip off the water, rinse the bones and re-cover with cold water. Return to the boil over a high heat. Once it just comes to the boil immediately reduce the heat to a bare simmer. Skim the surface from time to time as necessary; simmer for 30 minutes then add the vegetables. Simmer for a further 1 hour then add the herbs, simmer for a further 30 minutes. Strain the stock through a very fine wire conical sieve or through muslin and cool as quickly as is possible.
</p>
